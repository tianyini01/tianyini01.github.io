Phonology · Phonetics · Sound change

Tianyi Ni

I am a Ph.D. student in Linguistics at The Ohio State University. I study how phonetic cues such as duration and F0 become organized into prosodic grammar, including tone, quantity, tonal accent, moraic structure, and metrical contrasts.

My work combines formal phonological analysis with acoustic data, experiments, corpus evidence, and computational models of sound change. I am especially interested in why some prosodic systems are possible but typologically rare.
