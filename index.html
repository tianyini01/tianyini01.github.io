About me

I am a second year Ph.D student in the Department of Linguistics at The Ohio State. My main focus is on phonology and its interfaces with other aspects. I was trained as a software engineer and mathematician before I decided to move on to Linguistics, as a scientist. I also worked for two years as an engineer. I am multilingual. My mother tongues are Mandarin Chinese and Lower Yangtze Mandarin (淮安话). I am also fluent in Spanish, and German.
  • Both my first and last names are Mandarin Chinese.
  • You will probably see some computer science papers, under Tian-Yi Ni. That is also me.

Research interests

  • Broad: Phonology, theoretical (generative phonology theory, emergent phonology theory, etc) and laboratorial (psychological, computational, statistical, etc)
  • Narrow: Suprasegmental phenomena and their interactions with others
  • Diachronic language changes (sound change, syntactic change)
  • Phonetic encoding of morphological structure
  • Understudied languages, such as indigenous languages on different continents, southeastern Asian languages, etc
